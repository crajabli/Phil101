Arthur Schopenhauer

We can attain to the essence in itself... only upon the entirely different path that I have struck, by way of bringing in self-consiousness, which informs us of will as the in itself o f phenomenon.

We are not merely the knowing subject, but that we ourselves are among those relaities or entitites we require to know, that we oursleves are the thign in itself. Consequently, a way from within stands open to us that real inner nature of things to which we cannot penertrate from without.

  • the thing in itself - our essential being - is not condition by time and space
